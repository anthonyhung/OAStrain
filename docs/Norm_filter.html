Conclusions about normalizations: 1) There isn’t much of a difference between the outputs of each normalization method in terms of correlation heatmaps or number of genes kept after filtering for lowly expressed genes. 2) The driver for the differences between correlation heatmaps between the raw and filtered data comes down to the filtering of lowly expressed genes. In all cases, simply performing normalization and plotting the heatmap (without filtering out any genes) leads to a correlation heatmap that resembles the raw data’s heatmap quite closely (and in the case of pearson correlation, one that is even better matched up to desired structure between samples).
